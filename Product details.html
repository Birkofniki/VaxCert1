Product details

  1. VaxCert would create a blockchain-based platform to issue and verify digital vaccination certificates. These certificates would be stored on an immutable ledger as non-fungible tokens (NFTs) linked to an individual's verified identity.
  2. Individuals would obtain a VaxCert NFT after receiving a vaccine dose from an authorized provider.
    The NFT would contain details like vaccine type, batch, date administered, etc.
  3. Verifiers such as border agents, venue staff, employers could instantly check someone's vaccination status by scanning their VaxCert NFT.
    The blockchain would provide a tamper-proof record of immunizations.

Subscription Fees

Here is a proposed subscription fee table for the VaxCert software:

User Type Fee
Individual Free
Government Agencies $50,000/year
Healthcare Providers $20/month
Airlines $0.10 per verification
Venues/Events $150/month
Employers $5/employee per month

Key points:

  • Individuals can use the basic VaxCert wallet app for free. Revenue is generated from institutional users.
  • Government agencies would pay a flat annual fee to issue certified vaccination NFTs to residents.
  • Healthcare providers would pay a monthly subscription to mint VaxCert tokens after administering vaccines.
  • Airlines and venues would be charged per verification to validate passenger/attendee vaccination status.
  • Employers would pay a monthly fee per employee to verify vaccine status for staff.
  • Transaction fees on the blockchain could provide additional revenue on top of subscriptions.
  • Tiered pricing plans could also be offered for high volume users like airlines, venues.
  • Free or discounted access could be offered to health NGOs and partners in developing countries.
